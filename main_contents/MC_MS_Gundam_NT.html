Characters


Jona Basta

Jona Basta is the main protagonist of Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative. He is the pilot of the RX-9 Narrative Gundam.

A 25-year-old Ensign in the Earth Federation, Jona has been assigned to the Shezarr unit of the Earth Federation Forces Clop-class cruiser Damascus as part of the "Phoenix Hunt" taskforce at the request of the Luio & Co.[1]

Jona lost both his parents sometime during the One Year War. Due to the loss of records during the chaos of the war, the cause was undetermined. As such Jona grew up in foster care in Australia alongside Rita Bernal and Michele Luio, up until the Operation British colony drop, to which Rita had foreseen and warned about. After saving an entire town, they were dubbed the "Miracle Children".[3]

Following these events, the "Miracle Children" were sent to a Newtype testing facilities ran by Titans. After Michele's claim that Rita was the true Newtype, Michele was taken by Luio & Co. and Jona was released at the end of Gryps War, when the facility was shut down. Jona was subsequently able to obfuscate his past by erasing his records.[3]

Official Federation records claimed Jona was adopted by a North American family and enrolled into a EFSF military academy at age 18, graduated at 22, and was commissioned as an Ensign.[3]

Following their separation, Jona was able to reunite with Michele via her connections with Luio & Co.

During the final confrontation against Zoltan Akkanen and his NZ-999 II Neo Zeong, Jona is able to board the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex and awakens as a Newtype, activating its NT-D System.

Zoltan Akkanen

Zoltan Akkanen is one of the main antagonists of Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative. He is the pilot of the MSN-06S-2 Sinanju Stein.

A Lieutenant in the Republic of Zeon army, he aims to capture the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex and is the pilot of the MSN-06S-2 Sinanju Stein. Like Full Frontal, the leader of the Neo Zeon remnants, the Sleeves, he is a Cyber Newtype created as a second coming of Char Aznable. However, he is deemed to be a failure - a fact he is extremely insecure about. Because of this, he has an unrelenting need to prove himself to be worthy of the title.[1]

Zoltan has shown to be erratic, sadistic, and completely insane. He has shown little regard for the live of others, as he did not hesitate to fire his beam weapons in a space colony. His psychotic nature shows when he knowingly killed civilians with said beam weapons, while shooting at Jona Basta. He went as far as to use his senseless slaughter and destruction in an attempt to goad Jona into fighting back.[1]

His destructive and insane behavior seems to stem heavily from his insecurities; even minuscule implications of this fact are enough to sent Zoltan into fits of rage. Examples of this behavior include his first engagement with Jona, when he noticed Jona is a Cyber Newtype who isn't using the RX-9 Narrative Gundam to its fullest extent. Jona was unaware of the Narrative's full potential (in fact, Jona was actively trying to limit collateral damage in the colony), but Zoltan perceived Jona's behavior as a mockery from the opposing side, that his capabilities didn't justify the deployment of a better pilot or mobile suit. Another example of his insanity would be when he successfully used his Newtype powers to control the NZ-999 II Neo Zeong's Psycho-Frame to grab, throw, crush, and detonate a large Helium-3 tank. The massive destruction resulted in Zoltan laughing ecstatically.[1]

Rita Bernal

Rita Bernal is a character in Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative. She is the pilot of the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex.

Rita shared a similar childhood to Jona Basta, growing up as an orphan in Australia after losing her parents during the One Year War. During a school trip in U.C. 0079, she foresaw the occurrence of Operation British and its failure which would result in a colony hitting Sydney. She, along with her childhood friends Jona and Michele Luio, were able to save an entire town by warning them of the upcoming tragedy. Due to this, the three became known as the "Miracle Children".[2]

In U.C. 0095, she served as the pilot of the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex and went missing after the Phenex ran wild during an activation test. Her soul later merged with the Phenex's Psycho-Frame. The Phenex, containing Rita's soul, reappeared a year and a half later in U.C.0097.[2]

Michele Luio

Michele Luio is a character in Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative.

A 25-year-old Special Advisor to Luio & Co., Michele has been assigned to the Schezar unit of the Earth Federation Forces cruiser Damascus as part of the "Phoenix Hunter" taskforce at the request of the Intelligence Bureau. Michele is willing to do anything to achieve her goals even handing over the NZ-999 II Neo Zeong to the Sleeves just to draw out the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex, despite the dangers. She is also highly manipulative seeing Jona as a tool though she comes to regret it.

Martha Vist Carbine

Martha Vist Carbine is a powerful businesswoman and one of the main antagonists of Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn. During the events of the series, she seizes control of the Vist Foundation from her brother, Cardeas Vist, and oversees a destructive campaign to reclaim Laplace's Box.

A woman with a somewhat elegant appearance, shoulder length blonde hair, tight cheeks and deep eyes that give off a cold magnetism. She is married to the chairman of Anaheim Electronics, Melanie Hue Carbine. Martha Vist is often referred to as "The Empress Of The Moon", referring to her position at the head of the Carbine family, which established Anaheim Electronics. Through the Carbine family she has connections with the higher ups of the Earth Federation. She has a sharper mouth than her husband, which makes her a shrewd businesswoman. To the Vist family, it was a rarity to bear a woman of such character.

Iago Haakana

Iago Haakana is a character from the Mobile Suit Gundam UC: Phoenix Hunting novel and the Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative film.

Iago led the Shezarr Unit's RGM-96Xs Jesta (Shezarr Type)team in capturing the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex in Side 4. However, even after multiple attempts, the team still could not capture the Phenex. Iago then instructed his snipers to target the Phenex's thrusters to immobilise it, but that failed as well. Not long after, he received a message from an unknown source, ordering him to scatter his team and regroup at designated point Oscar. Initally, Iago demanded the unidentified caller to state their affiliation, but he reluctantly complied after being told that his teams' safety could be in danger if he did not. The Jestas spread out just in time to dodge the High Mega Cannon beam from the RX-9/A Narrative Gundam A-Packs. Later, seeing how the Narrative was struggling to capture the Phenex, Iago instructed the rest of his team to provide help. However, after hearing Rita Bernal's voice from the Phenex, the Narrative's pilot, Jona Basta, decided to fire his High Mega Cannon at the Jestas to allow the Phenex to escape. Once again, Iago ordered his team to scatter, losing the Phenex in the process.

Back at the Damascus, Iago questioned Jona as he found the latter's background to be suspicious. During their argument, Jona dropped his lucky charm, which contains a fragment of the necklace that his father wore. Iago caught sight of it and wondered why he had it. Later, the Shezarr Unit's Jestas participated in a mock battle with the RX-9/B Narrative Gundam B-Packs. During the battle, one of Iago's subordinates noted that Jona's skills as a mobile suit pilot was average, and Iago remarked that it seemed Jona was not enhanced.
The Damascus then travelled to the colony "Metis", where the Phenex was supposedly detected. As the ship stayed outside the colony, the Jestas and Narrative Gundam B-Packs managed to enter it using Luio & Co.'s name. Inside the colony, Iago reminded his team that weapons fire here is forbidden and that they should herd the Phenex to the nearest docking bay when they found it. When one of the Jestas detected something in the sky, Jona rushed ahead and ignored Iago's attempts to call him back. As the Narrative Gundam B-Packs engaged with the MSN-06S-2 Sinanju Stein, Iago and his subordinates battled several AMS-129 Geara Zulu (Guards Type)s. When the Narrative's NT-D activated and it docked with the NZ-999 II Neo Zeong, Iago tried to get closer to talk some sense into Jona, but was blown away by a mysterious power. He then noted that this power felt similar to the Axis Shock that pushed the asteroid base, Axis, away from Earth several years prior. Jona later calmed down after hearing Rita's voice from the Phenex, and Iago retreated with his team back to the Damascus.

During the final battle at the helium-3 storage base, Iago sortied in his Jesta to help Jona and the Phenex against the II Neo Zeong. He believed that Jona and the Phenex could defeat the enemy, and revealed that he was there when the RX-93 Î½ Gundam was trying to push back Axis. His unit was then hijacked by the II Neo Zeong, and he ordered Jona to shoot him down, but the latter refused. Iago's Jesta was freed when Michele Luio's Base Jabber exploded and landed on the wire used for hijacking his machine, he then retreated from the battle. After the battle ended, Iago stood on one of three Jestas' hand as the team arrived to pick up Jona.

Mechs


RX-9 Narrative Gundam

The RX-9 Narrative Gundam is the titular mobile suit of Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative and it is piloted by Jona Basta.

A mobile suit made by Anaheim Electronics, it was developed as a prototype for the RX-93 Î½ Gundam, testing the Psycho-Frame, and many portions of its body are made from identical parts.[1][2] Because it was intended mainly as a testbed for data collection, there are many areas that have not been equipped with armor, leaving the frame structure exposed.[1][2] The base machine is armed only with head vulcans, and during Operation Phoenix Hunt it uses a variety of interchangeable armament provided by Luio & Co.[1][2] The Narrative's cockpit lacks the standard 360 degree panoramic monitor, and is located in the Core Block system that can detach and change into a Core Fighter during emergency. It is also secretly fitted with the Newtype Destroyer System (NT-D) for the operation.

MSN-06S-2 Sinanju Stein

The MSN-06S-2 Sinanju Stein is a mobile suit from Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative and is piloted by Zoltan Akkanen.

The MSN-06S Sinanju Stein was developed to test the durability and follow-up capabilities of the Psycho-Frame.[1] Two units of the Mobile Suit, referred to using the development code "Stein 01", were later stolen by the Neo Zeon remnants, the Sleeves.[1][2] One unit was remodeled as the MSN-06S Sinanju and used as the flagship MS of the organization.[1][3] In UC 0097, the other unit was seen in the fight over the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex and was embellished with the engravings that became root for the name of The Sleeves.[1][3]

RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 (Phenex)

The RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex[1] (aka Phenex) is a mobile suit introduced in Mobile Suit Gundam UC: One of Seventy Two. It also appeared in the manga Mobile Suit Gundam U.C. 0096: Last Sun and the Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn novel. It made its animated debut in the movie Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative. Known pilots included Rita Bernal and Jona Basta.

The RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex was a new machine secretly built under the direction of a E.F.S.F. official who disapproved of the Vist Foundation's involvement in the UC Project. Using a psycho-frame body delivered in advance, it was assembled independently by the Earth Federation Forces and reflects the data of Units 1 and 2. Rather than that of a Unicorn, the Phenex's motif is that of a phoenix, illustrated with a much more elaborate horn/V-fin and a golden body.

ARX-014S Silver Bullet Suppressor

The ARX-014S Silver Bullet Suppressor is a variant of the ARX-014 Silver Bullet from Mobile Suit Gundam Narrative and is piloted by Banagher Links.

The ARX-014S Silver Bullet Suppressor is a remodeled version of a ARX-014 Silver Bullet stored in the Magallanica.[1][2] The main reason for the remodeling was to enable it to use the RX-0 series' Beam Magnum.[1] To accomplish this, its right arm is modified to be exchangeable, its backpack is changed to one that stores four replacement arms and an arm exchange system consisting of a crane and rail is installed on the rear waist armor.[1][2] These measures allow the exchange of the right arm after each shot of the Beam Magnum, countering the malfunctions that occurred to the arm due to its intense recoil.[1][2]

Other notable modifications include its face being greatly different, the number of antennas are increased to a total of six, modified knees and new feet.[1][2] All of the modifications are possible due to the high extensibility of the original Silver Bullet.[1] The colors of the mobile suit have also been changed to a black color scheme.[1][2] Besides the Beam Magnum, the Silver Bullet Suppressor's other armaments consisted of 60mm Vulcan Guns, Beam Sabers, Grenade Launchers and Beam Cannons.[2] The "Suppressor" in its name is said to signify that "the mobile suit is to deter and restrain hostile organizations".[1]

RAS-96 Anksha

The RAS-96 Anksha is a mass-production transformable mobile suit introduced in Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn.

The RAS-96 Anksha was developed as the successor to the NRX-044 Asshimar, but shares many interior components with the RGM-89 Jegan. This enhanced maintenance and production efficiency, as well as reduced manufacturing cost. Like the Asshimar, the Anksha is capable of high-altitude atmospheric flight without the help of a sub-flight system while in its mobile armor mode. With the application of magnetic-coating technology on its joints and moving parts, the Anksha can transform between mobile suit and mobile armor modes in a matter of seconds. The Anksha was able to serve as a sub-flight system for other mobile suits in the same fashion as a Base Jabber. It was also able to respond flexibly to high-altitude interceptor missions.

Unlike the Asshimar and more like Federation suits of the era, the Anksha abandons its predecessor's mono-eye sensor in favor of the typical compound sensor unit as seen on the Jegan and RGZ-95 ReZEL. Similar to the Asshimar, the head is equipped with internal differential pressure sensors and air traffic control radar. Other enhancements made to the Anksha from the Asshimar's original design included a larger number of stabilizers and flight-surfaces, increasing the unit's stability during flight. Interestingly, the suit also incorporated technology from ORX-005 Gaplant in the form of the forearm-mounted movable shield binders which also contain the suit's beam rifles.

MSK-008 Dijeh

The MSK-008 Dijeh is a mobile suit introduced in the anime Mobile Suit Zeta Gundam. During the Gryps War, it was piloted by Amuro Ray.

The Dijeh is a prototype ground-use mobile suit developed by Karaba (hence the 'K' in its model number), the earthbound counterpart of the AEUG, based on the RMS-099 Rick Dias that AEUG left behind after the assault on Jaburo. Karaba put all of its industrial capabilities and technologies supplied by AEUG into building the suit, and although it uses avionics from the AEUG's MSA-003 Nemo, its specifications are far better and worthy of an ace-use machine. Like the Rick Dias, many former Zeon engineers were involved in the Dijeh's development, hence its design has many Zeon-style features, such as the mono-eye sensor. Another similarity with the Rick Dias is that the Dijeh's cockpit is also located in its head.

To adapt to ground operations, the suit's cooling system is improved and lightened. Furthermore, large heat radiator fins are mounted on its back, and the suit can hover over the ground due to the vectoring nozzle type jet thrusters installed in the legs. The suit also features enhanced communication capabilities due to the blade antenna on the head and rod-shaped multi-antennas (also known as radar receivers) mounted on top of the heat radiator fins. Amuro Ray, Karaba's ace and eventual pilot of the Dijeh, participated in the suit's development at the final stage and suggested the weapons rack on the left shoulder. Armament-wise, the suit has a pair of vulcan guns, a beam naginata, a shield on the right shoulder, and uses the beam rifle used by the AEUG's MSN-00100 Hyaku Shiki, as well as the Rick Dias' clay bazooka. It can also use the same hyper mega launcher as the MSZ-006 Zeta Gundam.

In U.C.0097, Luio & Co. operated a number of dark gray-colored Dijehs that were equipped with beam rifles modified to use the same standard E-pac as the beam rifles of RGM-89D Jegan D Type and RGZ-95 ReZEL. Additionally, the commander-use unit has a searchlight on its left shoulder.

Factions


Shezzar Unit

An elite mobile suit team of the Earth Federation Forces, the Shezarr Team was based on the Clop-class cruiser Damascus. In U.C. 0097, the team was given a top secret assignment by the General Staff Headquarters: the capture of the RX-0 Unicorn Gundam 03 Phenex, which went missing in U.C. 0095, but unexpectedly resurfaced after the Laplace Declaration. For this mission, the entire team was outfitted with customized Jesta outfitted with special equipment.[1]

The team eventually encountered and engaged the Phenex in the shoal zone near Side 4, but the Phenex evaded all of the team's attempts to capture it. They were suddenly joined in the pursuit by Jona Basta in the RX-9/A Narrative Gundam A-Packs. Eventually, Jona got close enough to the Phenex to launch a communication wire at it, causing the Narrative to be dragged along by the Phenex.[1]

Shezarr Unit's team leader, Iago Haakana, asked Jona to drag the Phenex to the designated point. However, the voice of Rita Bernal suddenly began emanate from the Phenex, much to the shock of everyone present. In response, Jona severed the wire and fired the Narrative's mega particle cannon at it, only to miss and allow the Phenex to escape.[1]

Republic of Zeon

The Republic of Zeon is a nation featured in the Mobile Suit Zeta Gundam television series and the Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn OVA. It existed before the Principality of Zeon and later would become its successor state.

In U.C.0053, Zeon Zum Deikun was appointed Prime Minister of Side 3. On September 14, U.C.0058, Side 3 declared independence and established the Republic of Zeon. In U.C.0068, Deikun suddenly died of a heart attack during a parliamentary speech. When Degwin Sodo Zabi took control, he wiped out the opposition and abolished the Republic on August 15, 0069, and declared a transition to a monarchy. The Zabi family, having gained power, aimed for independence from the Federation government by force. On January, U.C. 0079, the Zeon War of Independence (One Year War) began. With the destruction of the Zabi family during the closing stages of the One Year War, the Principality of Zeon transitioned to a Republic just before the end of the war and returned to its name of Republic of Zeon. During the First Neo Zeon War in U.C.0088, Side 3 along with the Republic, came under control of Neo Zeon and would eventually be liberated by the AEUG and Federation fleet after the self-destruction of Neo Zeon.

Following the One Year War, the Republic of Zeon was created with a pro-Earth Federation government led by Darcia Bakharo. Although regarded by some as a Federation puppet government, the Republic had been granted a degree of autonomy. However, the Federation planned to dissolve the Republic by U.C. 0100, which would cause the name of Zeon and his Newtype theory to fade into obscurity. Republic of Zeon defense minister Monaghan Bakharo would devise the Co-Prosperity Sphere plan and sponsored the Neo Zeon remnants movement, known as the Sleeves, led by Full Frontal to continue the fight for Spacenoid independence. When the Vist Foundation offered them Laplace's Box, an enigmatic object that had the power to topple the Federation government, Frontal intended to use it as a time saver to forestall the dissolution of the Republic so it could help in the formation of the Side Co-Prosperity Sphere, which would boycott the Earth, forcing mass space migration and causing the Federation government to collapse. However, following the defeat of the Sleeves in the Laplace Incident, the Republic of Zeon retired their independence in U.C. 0100 and was folded back into the Earth Federation.

Luio & Co.

Luio & Co. is a faction introduced in the anime Mobile Suit Zeta Gundam

Luio & Co. is a large enterprise based in New Hong Kong. it has a close relations with the Earth Federation government, and is a competitor of Vist Foundation.[1]
